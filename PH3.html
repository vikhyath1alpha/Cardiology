Executive Summary

Pulmonary hypertension (PH) is defined by mPAP > 20 mmHg (2022 update). Correct classification (Groups 1-5) dictates therapy. Goals: achieve and maintain low-risk status using multiparametric risk tools.

  • Pre-capillary PH: mPAP >20, PAWP ≤15, PVR ≥2 WU.
  • Post-capillary PH: mPAWP >15 mmHg; distinguish Ipc vs Cpc.
  • First-line PAH: dual oral (ERA + PDE5i); high-risk add prostacyclin.
  • CTEPH: PEA curative; riociguat/BPA for inoperable disease.

1.2 Clinical Groups (6th WSPH)

  • Group 1 – PAH: IPAH, HPAH (BMPR2), drugs/toxins, CTD, CHD, etc.
  • Group 2 – PH-LHD: HFpEF/HFrEF; Ipc-PH vs Cpc-PH.
  • Group 3 – Lung Disease: COPD, ILD, sleep-disordered breathing.
  • Group 4 – CTEPH: Organized thrombi; PEA curative.
  • Group 5 – Unclear/Multifactorial: Hemolytic anemias, sarcoidosis.

2. Diagnostic Algorithm

  1. Suspicion: dyspnea, loud P₂, TR murmur, edema.
  2. TTE: probability based on TRV >2.8 m/s + ancillary signs.
  3. V/Q scan: rule-out CTEPH (normal scan excludes CTEPH).
  4. RHC: confirm mPAP >20 mmHg; vasoreactivity in Group 1 only.
  5. Group-specific workup: CTD labs, HIV, HRCT, etc.

4. Therapeutic Strategies by Group

4.1 Group 1 – PAH

Initial Low/Intermediate Risk

  • ERA (ambrisentan/macitentan)
  • + PDE5i (sildenafil/tadalafil)
  • OR sGC stimulator (riociguat)

High Risk

  • IV prostacyclin (epoprostenol)
  • + ERA + PDE5i
  • Consider sotatercept (2024)
Key Trials: AMBITION (dual), GRIPHON (selexipag), STELLAR (sotatercept).

4.2 Group 2 – PH-LHD

Treat underlying left heart disease; avoid PAH drugs in Ipc-PH. Cpc-PH may enter trials.

4.3 Group 3 – Lung Disease

Optimize lung therapy; inhaled treprostinil approved for PH-ILD (INCREASE trial).

4.4 Group 4 – CTEPH

  • PEA: curative for surgically accessible disease.
  • Inoperable: riociguat, macitentan, or BPA.

5. Board-Ready Key Points

  • Know the mPAP >20 mmHg & PVR ≥2 WU thresholds.
  • Always perform V/Q scan to exclude CTEPH.
  • Goal: achieve low-risk profile with multiparametric tools.
  • Initial PAH therapy: dual oral combination; high-risk add prostacyclin.
  • CTEPH: PEA first-line; BPA/riociguat for inoperable.
  • Group 2 Ipc-PH: no PAH drugs; optimize LHD therapy.
