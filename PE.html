Current Risk Stratification and Guidelines

The 2019 ESC/ERS Guidelines

The 2019 ESC/ERS Guidelines are the foundation for pulmonary embolism management, emphasizing a comprehensive risk stratification approach. The classification system categorizes patients into four risk groups:

  • • **Low-risk PE**: Hemodynamically stable with PESI class I-II or sPESI = 0
  • • **Intermediate-low risk PE**: Hemodynamically stable with PESI class III-IV or sPESI $\geq$1, but without both RV dysfunction and elevated cardiac biomarkers
  • • **Intermediate-high risk PE**: Hemodynamically stable with PESI class III-IV or sPESI $\geq$1, plus both RV dysfunction and elevated cardiac biomarkers
  • • **High-risk PE**: Hemodynamically unstable with systolic blood pressure <90 mmHg or requiring vasopressor support

Mortality rates by risk category:

  • • Low-risk: 0.7%
  • • Intermediate-low: 2.3%
  • • Intermediate-high: 3.4-12.6%
  • • High-risk: 30-60%

Advanced Risk Stratification Tools

Novel Risk Classification Systems

The **RISA-PE classification (2024)** adapts SCAI shock stages to PE, providing more granular risk stratification:

  1. **Stage A:** RV dysfunction + troponin elevation (mortality: 1.2%)
  2. **Stage B:** Stage A + lactate >2 mmol/L OR shock index $\geq$1 (mortality: 6.4%)
  3. **Stage C:** Persistent hypotension (mortality: 19.0%)
  4. **Stage D:** Obstructive shock (mortality: 25.6%)
  5. **Stage E:** Cardiac arrest (mortality: 57.7%)

Biomarker Integration

**Neutrophil-to-lymphocyte ratio (NLR)** and **platelet-to-lymphocyte ratio (PLR)** show promise as prognostic markers.

When integrated with ESC risk stratification, these biomarkers improved predictive accuracy (AUC 0.848 vs 0.771 for ESC stratification alone).

Diagnostic Advances

D-dimer Optimization

The age-adjusted D-dimer cutoff has been validated to improve diagnostic efficiency:

  • • Standard cutoff: <500 µg/L for all patients
  • • Age-adjusted cutoff: <500 µg/L for patients $\leq$50 years, <(age $\times$ 10) µg/L for patients >50 years

This approach increased the proportion of elderly patients (>75 years) in whom PE could be ruled out from 6.4% to 29.7%.

Biomarker Thresholds

Critical laboratory values for risk stratification:

  • • **Troponin I:**
    • Low-risk threshold: <12 pg/mL
    • High-risk threshold: 87 pg/mL (in patients with sPESI $\geq$1 or RV dysfunction)
  • • **NT-proBNP:** $\geq$600 pg/mL indicates increased mortality risk
  • • **Lactate:** >2 mmol/L associated with worse outcomes

Echocardiographic Assessment

Key Parameters for Right Ventricular Assessment:

  • • **RV/LV ratio:** >1.0 indicates RV dysfunction
  • • **TAPSE:** <17 mm associated with worse outcomes
  • • **TAPSE/PASP ratio:**
    • <0.40 (low-risk), <0.43 (intermediate-low), <0.34 (intermediate-high) associated with increased mortality
  • • **RV global longitudinal strain (RVGLS):** $\leq$17.7% indicates dysfunction
  • The combination of RVGLS and RV/LV ratio provides superior prognostic value, with patients having high RVGLS (>17.7%) and low RV/LV ratio (<1.03) showing 99.0% 30-day survival.

Catheter-Directed Interventions: The New Paradigm

PEERLESS Trial (2024) - Practice-Changing Results

The **PEERLESS trial** represents the largest randomized controlled trial comparing catheter-based interventions. In 550 patients with intermediate-risk PE:

  • • Primary endpoint (hierarchical win ratio): 5.01 (95% CI: 3.68-6.97, p<0.001) favoring large-bore mechanical thrombectomy (LBMT) over catheter-directed thrombolysis (CDT).

Key findings:

  • • Clinical deterioration/bailout: 1.8% (LBMT) vs 5.4% (CDT), p=0.04
  • • ICU utilization: 41.6% (LBMT) vs 98.6% (CDT) for admissions
  • • Hospital stay: 4.5$\pm$2.8 vs 5.3$\pm$3.9 days
  • • 30-day readmission: 3.2% vs 7.9%
  • • No difference in mortality or major bleeding

Ultrasound-Assisted Thrombolysis (USAT)

**KNOCOUT PE Registry (2024)** demonstrated excellent safety profile:

  • • Mean alteplase dose: 18.1 mg (31% received $\leq$12 mg)
  • • Major bleeding: 1.6% at 72 hours
  • • 30-day mortality: 1.0%
  • • Significant improvement in RV function and quality of life

Mechanical Thrombectomy Systems

FlowTriever System outcomes:

  • • FLASH registry (800 patients): 30-day mortality 0.8%
  • • FLAME study: 1.9% mortality in high-risk PE patients
  • • Technical success: 100% across multiple studies
  • • Immediate hemodynamic improvement: 23% reduction in mean PAP

Safety considerations:

Analysis of FDA MAUDE database revealed:

  • • Most common complications: Pulmonary vascular perforation (26 cases), right heart injury/tamponade (9 cases)
  • • Mortality events: 26 (FlowTriever) vs 28 (Penumbra Indigo)

Anticoagulation Management

DOAC Preferences

Rivaroxaban and apixaban are preferred as monotherapy for acute PE:

  • • **Rivaroxaban:** 15 mg BID $\times$ 21 days, then 20 mg daily
  • • **Apixaban:** 10 mg BID $\times$ 7 days, then 5 mg BID

Comparative effectiveness: Apixaban showed lower risk of recurrent VTE (HR 0.37, 95% CI: 0.24-0.55) and major bleeding (HR 0.54, 95% CI: 0.37-0.82) compared to rivaroxaban.

Heparin Considerations

LMWH vs UFH meta-analysis found no significant differences in:

  • • Short-term mortality: OR 0.89 (95% CI: 0.44-1.82)
  • • Long-term mortality: OR 0.77 (95% CI: 0.50-1.21)
  • • Major bleeding: OR 0.70 (95% CI: 0.38-1.32)

Intermediate-Risk PE: The Management Dilemma

Systemic Thrombolysis

Half-dose thrombolysis (50 mg alteplase) shows promise:

  • • Improved RV-arterial coupling: TAPSE/PASP ratio improvement
  • • Reduced RV dimensions: -6.00 mm reduction in RVD1
  • • Acceptable bleeding risk: 3.5% major bleeding rate

Meta-analysis findings: Systemic thrombolysis in intermediate-risk PE showed:

  • • No mortality benefit: Similar mortality rates
  • • Reduced need for escalation: Lower rates of vasopressor use and rescue therapy
  • • Increased ICH risk: Higher intracranial hemorrhage rates

Catheter-Directed Therapy Selection

Real-world comparative analysis (162 patients):

  • • Primary composite endpoint: 11.2% (CBT) vs 15.2% (CDT)
  • • Major bleeding: 7.8% (CBT) vs 17.4% (CDT), p=0.042
  • • Hemodynamic deterioration: 5% (CBT) vs 21.7% (CDT), p=0.036

Long-Term Sequelae

Chronic Thromboembolic Pulmonary Hypertension (CTEPH)

**COMMAND VTE Registry-2 (2024)** reported:

  • • Cumulative incidence: 2.3% at 3 years post-PE
  • • Risk factors: Female gender (HR 2.09), longer symptom-to-diagnosis interval, hypoxemia, RV dysfunction, unprovoked PE

CTEPH screening recommendations:

  • • Routine follow-up: 3-6 months post-PE for symptomatic patients
  • • Imaging: Echocardiography, V/Q scan, or CTPA
  • • Hemodynamic assessment: Right heart catheterization if suspected

Post-PE Syndrome

Prevalence: 40-60% of PE survivors

Components: Persistent dyspnea, exercise intolerance, impaired functional status after $\geq$3 months of adequate anticoagulation

Neurophysiological mechanisms: Post-PE patients demonstrate:

  • • Higher inspiratory neural drive during exercise
  • • Impaired gas exchange: Reduced DLCO (84$\pm$15 vs 104$\pm$14% predicted)
  • • Reduced exercise capacity: Peak VO2 (76$\pm$14 vs 124$\pm$28% predicted)

PERT Programs: Multidisciplinary Approach

PERT implementation benefits:

  • • Advanced therapy utilization: 23.2-fold higher odds of receiving interventional therapy
  • • Improved outcomes: Despite higher severity, similar mortality rates
  • • Streamlined care: Reduced time to intervention

Follow-up protocols:

  • • Initial visit: 2 weeks to 3 months post-PE
  • • Assessment focus: Anticoagulation management, symptom evaluation, complication screening
  • • Long-term monitoring: CTEPH surveillance, functional assessment

Emerging Therapies and Future Directions

Novel Interventional Approaches

**3D-guided percutaneous thrombectomy** shows promise with:

  • • 100% technical success in 16 patients
  • • Significant hemodynamic improvement: Reduced PAP and improved oxygenation
  • • Low complication rates: No major bleeding or procedural mortality

Biomarker Research

**CLOT-5 score (2024)** demonstrated superior predictive accuracy:

  • • AUC: 0.901 vs 0.793 (PESI) and 0.728 (sPESI)
  • • Incorporates: Vital signs, biomarkers, and imaging data

Key Numerical Thresholds for Clinical Practice

Risk Stratification Cutoffs

  • • **sPESI:** 0 (low-risk), $\geq$1 (intermediate-risk)
  • • **PESI:** >175.5 indicates high mortality risk
  • • **Shock index:** $\geq$1.0 indicates higher risk

Biomarker Thresholds

  • • **D-dimer:** Age-adjusted >50 years: (age $\times$ 10) µg/L
  • • **Troponin I:** <12 pg/mL (low-risk), >87 pg/mL (high-risk with RV dysfunction)
  • • **NT-proBNP:** $\geq$600 pg/mL (increased mortality risk)
  • • **Lactate:** >2 mmol/L (associated with poor outcomes)

Echocardiographic Cutoffs

  • • **RV/LV ratio:** >1.0 (RV dysfunction)
  • • **TAPSE:** <17 mm (RV dysfunction)
  • • **TAPSE/PASP:** <0.40 (low-risk), <0.43 (intermediate-low), <0.34 (intermediate-high)
  • • **RVGLS:** $\leq$17.7% (RV dysfunction)
